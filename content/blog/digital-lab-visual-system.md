## Atmosphere is not noise

Terminal aesthetics can become a wall of green characters very quickly. That is memorable, but it is not always readable.

The better move is translation: keep terminal language in navigation, metadata, tags, and local components while the article body stays calm.

::callout{type="note" title="design rule"}
The homepage can be expressive. The article page should be quiet enough for a reader to stay.
::

## What the references contribute

`ein-code-digital-lab` is the best information architecture reference because it already has blog, notes, projects, and workbench surfaces.

`retro-crt-portfolio` contributes scanlines, warm CRT texture, and system chrome. Those details should appear as atmosphere, not as a reading constraint.

`8-bit-retro-blog` contributes command-line microcopy. It works best in small doses.

## Operating principles

- The homepage can carry the strongest visual signature.
- The list view should be easy to scan.
- The article page prioritizes line length, rhythm, and code blocks.
- Motion should clarify hierarchy, not interrupt reading.
